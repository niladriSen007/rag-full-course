Sir APJ Abdul Kalam - The Missile Man of India

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as APJ Abdul Kalam, was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007 and was widely regarded as the "People's President."

Early Life and Education:
Born into a humble family, Kalam showed exceptional academic prowess from an early age. He graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology.

Career and Contributions:
- Joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in 1958
- Moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 1969
- Played a pivotal role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998
- Led the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III)
- Instrumental in developing ballistic missiles like Agni and Prithvi
- Earned the title "Missile Man of India" for his work in missile technology

Awards and Honors:
- Padma Bhushan (1981)
- Padma Vibhushan (1990)
- Bharat Ratna (1997) - India's highest civilian honor
- Numerous honorary doctorates from universities worldwide

Philosophy and Vision:
Dr. Kalam was a strong advocate for education, youth empowerment, and technological advancement. His book "Wings of Fire" is an inspiration to millions. He envisioned India as a developed nation by 2020 through his "India 2020" vision.

Legacy:
APJ Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong. He remains an icon of simplicity, dedication, and patriotism, inspiring generations of Indians to dream big and work hard.
